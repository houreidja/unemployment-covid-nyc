Unemployment rates drop citywide one year after COVID lockdown

By Houreidja Tall

December 13, 2021

According to data from the New York State Department of Labor, unemployment in New York City has decreased since the beginning of the COVID lockdown as of April 2021. Each borough has shown a decrease in unemployment, with Queens showing the highest decrease overall.

In sets of data from April 2020 and 2021, Manhattan, the third most-populous borough, had the lowest rates of unemployment citywide, from 11.5% in April 2020 to 8.3% in April 2021. The Bronx, the fourth most-populated of the five boroughs, had the highest rates of unemployment from 18.5% to 15% over the same period of time.

This overall decrease in unemployment may be attributed to a number of reasons. One reason may be increased reopenings throughout the city after months of closures in 2020, which would have led to people gaining jobs.

This decrease in unemployment a little over one year after COVID first reached New York City is a promising sign that the city has been successfully figuring out how to recover its workforce in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic.
